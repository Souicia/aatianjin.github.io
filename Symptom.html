Three Stages

Early Stage (Stage 1)

  • Lasts 1~3 years
  • Most get diagnosed at this stage
  • Patients can continue to take part in social activities, and act independently
  • Have small problems with recalling recent events or communicating

Middle Stage (Stage 2)

  • Lasts 2~10 years
  • Patients now need more attention
  • Further memory loss
  • Patients lose their ability to understand time and space
  • Hard for the patients to go outside, dress up, and take care of personal hygiene on their own
  • Personality changes - more sensitive to physical and psychological stress
  • Start to go outside and wander, have frequent outbursts, and become depressed

Late Stage (Stage 3)

  • Lasts 1~2 years
  • Totally dependent on other individuals
  • Need care for 24 hours every day
  • Lose the ability to move themselves
  • Can't control their bowel movements or swallowing food
  • Lose the ability to communicate verbally
  • Not able to recognize close people
  • The problems will ultimately lead to death
